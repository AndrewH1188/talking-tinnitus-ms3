Welcome to Talking Tinnitus

We are glad to be able to welcome you to our community. We hope that by being an active member of our community this helps you to not only help others but also yourself at the same time.

We know and understand that tinnitus has different affects on different people. Tinnitus is common, its nothing to worry about and does get better over time, but needs to be managed not monitored. According to the British Tinnitus Association there are already an estimated six million people living with tinnitus here in the UK and this number is expected to rise to more than half a million by 2028.

Here at Talking Tinnitus we understand how tinnitus makes people feel, the different sounds, the intrusiveness that it has on peoples lives, but by Talking Tinnitus we can help to change the feelings towards it.

What is Tinnitus?

Tinnitus is the term for hearing a sound where no external sound is present. Tinnitus has many different sounds and ranges from whooshing, hissing, whistling, buzzing, humming, ringing, musical, pulsating, to name a few. This may be heard/experienced in one or both ears.

People with tinnitus experience all sorts of different sound levels, for some it is very loud and causes the individual to miss conversations and feel anxious, for some it can be a low level noise and can be heard when it is quiet, but for others it can be a combination of both at different times. Others report they have tinnitus that comes and goes, and doesn't really impact or affect their day to day life.

What can help?

There are many different things that you can do or try that will help you to manage your tinnitus, such as:
Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT), Meditation, Yoga, Mindfulness, Tai Chi, Relaxation, Exercising, Dancing, Sport, Acting and Drama.

There are many other activities and things you can do to help. These can be hobbies and interests, or even trying a new hobby or interest to help take your mind off the sound(s) that you are experiencing. These could include (but are not limited to):
Knitting, Sewing, Visualisation, CDs, Apps, Art, Painting, Lego, Construction Kits, Writing and even Talking to others with Tinnitus.
