About Us

Talking Tinnitus has been designed with you in mind. We want to connect everyone that has and is experiencing tinnitus to with each other. They may choose to write about how tinnitus affects them, what helps them, how they manage their tinnitus etc. This in turn will help others that have just started to experience tinnitus, or those that have had it for a while and need some help and suggestions from others of whom are also experiencing tinnitus to know they are not alone.

We have different categories so that you are able to easily upload your tips, tricks and thoughts, as well as find the categories that you are looking for. You control your experience and your profile and if there is something you find helpful please do share this on your social media and be sure to tag us in your post.

So lets head on over, sign up or sign in and lets Talk Tinnititus.

How we can help you?

We understand you as we have had tinnitus for a few years ourselves. We understand how it feels to experience this and what it is like to explain to others who don't understand. As a result of our understanding and being able to manage our tinnitus we have become a volunteer befriender with a national tinnitus service to help other people who also have tinnitus to help themselves.

We are all on this journey together. So lets with the help of our many active users take your hand and guide you to living with and managing your tinnitus, having a more fulfilling and happier life and lets look at tinnitus in a completely new way.

How you can help yourself?

You can manage your tinnitus but do not monitor it. Monitoring tinnitus creates a negative feeling towards it. When we have a negative feeling towards something this distresses us and can cause things like depression and anxiety. Acknowledge that the sound is there, maybe by saying something like “It's ok, I can hear you” then move on with what you are doing, but don't listen to it. Use sound, relaxation, exercise, board games or a hobby to help you take your mind off of it.

Join a tinnitus support group near you, or seek out organisations like the British Tinnitus Association or American Tinnitus Association. Talking to others who understand will also help you to help yourself.
